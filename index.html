Systems, not only tools

How I think & build

I like when AI systems behave like cities: many small agents, clear rules, smooth flows. Below is the stack I use to keep everything coherent.

AI & Data
From anomaly detection to language models.
  • LSTMs, transformers, embeddings.
  • RAG-style workflows & evaluation.
  • Fuzzy matching, regex, small heuristics.
Backend & APIs
Glue that lets AI breathe inside products.
  • Python, Flask, REST APIs.
  • SQLite, JSON stores, lightweight auth.
  • Integration with web / XR front-ends.
XR & Interaction
Embodied interfaces, not just screens.
  • Unity, C#, Oculus Quest.
  • Vuforia, AR-anchored information.
  • Comfort & safety in immersive spaces.
Human–Computer Interaction
Design research for calm technology.
  • User journeys & personas.
  • Usability tests with domain experts.
  • Design for low-friction cognition.
Agent-Based Thinking
Seeing systems as small interacting agents.
  • Simple rules → complex behaviour.
  • Digital twins & simulation ideas.
  • Resilience instead of brittle rules.
Collaboration
Working with engineers & non-technical teams.
  • Clear visuals, demos & prototypes.
  • Documentation that explains “why”.
  • Space for feedback & iteration.

Where this is heading

I’m especially interested in digital twins for rehabilitation and safety – systems where you can “feel” a complex process through a headset or a simple web interface and adjust it in real time.

I’d love to help research labs or companies that want to blend AI, XR and human-centred design into one coherent flow instead of three separate silos.

How I like to work

I enjoy slow, focused collaboration: whiteboard first, prototypes next, code last.

I’m comfortable switching between low-level details (data pipelines, API design) and high-level story (what this feels like for the user in a stressful situation).
